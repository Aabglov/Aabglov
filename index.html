How

Here's a demo I've been working on to improve GPS tracking on mobile devices. The dots above move about the area randomly while a neural network learns how to predict their next positions from their speed and direction. The squares represent where the network believes the dots to be based on the last location, direction and speed. To represent more realistic data there's also 20% random noise in both direction and speed.

I think this same technology could be applied to mobile sensors such as the accelerometer to reduce the reliance of location-tracking applications on GPS. This would result in improved battery life while using the apps and the ability to use apps in areas of weak GPS signal.

Special shout-out to Florian Boesch for the great tutorial on the floating dots and Synaptic for the machine learning framework.
